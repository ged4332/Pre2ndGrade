Your 2nd Grade Prep Companion

Welcome, Parents!

This guide provides a simple 6-week plan to help your child prepare for second grade. Each week is a themed adventure with fun, hands-on activities. Aim for 15-30 minutes of focused activity, 3-5 days a week.

Turn Learning into Play

The best learning happens when kids don't even realize it's happening! Encourage creativity, ask "what if" questions, and celebrate the fun of discovery. This isn't school, it's a summer adventure!

Unlocking Adventure Badges

At the end of each week's five missions, your child will earn a special badge that appears in their Adventure Log! The badge is awarded automatically when the final task is checked off.

Your Role as "Adventure Chief": Before checking that last box, this is a great moment to review the week's fun activities with your child. The "criteria" isn't perfection, but effort and enjoyment. Celebrate their creativity and hard work, and then check that final box together to unlock their reward!

Reading Adventure Resources

Encouraging daily reading is one of the best ways to support your child this summer! Here are some fun, age-appropriate resources you can use alongside our weekly missions.

From Your Local Library

Visit any Denver Public Library branch or use their website to find these wonderful book series. Many are available as e-books, too!

  • Funny Adventures: Look for the *Elephant & Piggie* series by Mo Willems or the *Mercy Watson* series by Kate DiCamillo.
  • Mysteries & Detectives: The *Nate the Great* series is a perfect match for our "Reading Detectives" week.
  • Amazing Animal Facts: Check out the *National Geographic Kids Readers (Level 1)* or the *Fly Guy Presents* series.
  • Classic Stories: You can't go wrong with the beloved *Frog and Toad* series by Arnold Lobel.

Library Tip: The secret to a great library trip is letting your child choose the books. When they are invested in the topic, reading becomes a treat, not a task.

Printable & Online Reading

For a quick, 15-minute activity, these free websites are fantastic:

  • ReadWorks.org: Create a free account to access hundreds of short, printable reading passages. You can search by "2nd Grade" to find the perfect options.
  • UniteforLiteracy.com: Explore a library of simple, free digital picture books on fun topics that are great for reading together on a screen.
